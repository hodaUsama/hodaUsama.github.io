Q: You have two datasets:

  1. 📦 Dataset A: {10, 12, 12, 13, 13, 13, 14, 15}
  2. 📦 Dataset B: {10, 12, 13, 14, 15, 70}

Both datasets have the same **median**. Which one has a larger range, and what does that tell you?

💡 Show Answer

✅ Dataset B has a much larger range (70 - 10 = 60) compared to Dataset A (15 - 10 = 5).
This tells us Dataset B has more extreme values — possibly an outlier.

Bonus: Why might the IQR be a better measure of dispersion than the range in some cases?

💡 Show Answer

✅ Because the IQR focuses on the middle 50% of the data and ignores outliers, making it more reliable when extreme values are present.
